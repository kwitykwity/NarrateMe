NarrateMe
Product Requirements Document: Net New Build
Build name: NarrateMe   |   Owner: Christina   |   Date: July 2026
1. Problem
Children in grades 1–3 struggle to stay engaged with static reading materials, and the teachers and parents who create content for them have no fast way to turn a plain written story into something more engaging, because doing so today means manually writing, illustrating, and narrating a story by hand or piecing together separate, disconnected AI tools. As a result, most stories stay flat text on a page, and kids who need more sensory engagement to stay focused and build comprehension don't get it.
Supporting Context
Static, text-only reading materials are a well-documented engagement barrier for early readers (grades 1–3), a stage where comprehension is closely tied to multisensory input.
No existing consumer tool takes a plain story and automatically produces an illustrated, narrated, multi-scene presentation in one step — the closest tools (AI image generators, TTS tools, avatar platforms) each solve one piece and require manual stitching between them.
Teachers and parents are increasingly expected to differentiate and personalize reading materials, but lack the design, illustration, or audio-production skills to do so themselves.
1a. Opportunity
Give any teacher or parent the ability to turn a story into an illustrated, narrated presentation in under a minute — no writing, illustration, or audio skills required — making personalized, multimedia reading material as easy to produce as pasting text into a box.
Market Opportunity
Early-literacy edtech and personalized children's media are active, growing categories, with demand from both classrooms and individual parents for tools that make reading more engaging without requiring production skills.
Adjacent markets (personalized children's books, AI narration/audiobook tools) are already proven, paying categories — NarrateMe combines their value into a single automated pipeline rather than a static, one-time product.
1b. Users & Needs
Primary user(s):
Teachers and parents of children in grades 1–3, who have a written story (their own, a student's, or an existing text) and want to make it more engaging without design or production skills.
Secondary users:
Children ages 6–9, who watch/listen to the resulting presentation as the end consumer of the content.
Key User Needs
As a teacher, I need to turn a plain story into an illustrated, narrated presentation because my students lose focus reading static text on a page.
As a parent, I need a fast way to make a story feel special and engaging because I don't have the time or skill to illustrate or narrate it myself.
As a teacher, I need the pipeline to work reliably without manual fixing because I don't have time to troubleshoot AI tools between classes.
2. Proposed Solution
NarrateMe is a web app that turns a written story into a narrated, illustrated, multi-scene presentation automatically. Users simply paste or type their story into a text box, and the system splits it into 3–5 scenes, generates a matching illustration for each scene, and narrates it with a warm, natural-sounding voice. As a result, teachers and parents can produce an engaging, presentation-ready story in under a minute, with no writing, illustration, or audio-editing skills required.
2a. Value Proposition
Teachers and parents who struggle with turning static, plain-text stories into something engaging for grades 1–3 readers use NarrateMe, a web app, to automatically generate an illustrated, narrated, multi-scene presentation from any written story. Unlike manually illustrating a story or stitching together separate AI image, voice, and design tools, NarrateMe delivers a complete, presentation-ready result in one step, helping educators and parents boost engagement without any design or production skill.
2b. Top 3 MVP Value Props
The Vitamin (must-have baseline):
A written story reliably becomes a clear, coherent, multi-scene illustrated presentation — the baseline table stakes without which the product has no reason to exist.
The Painkiller (solves the core pain):
No more manually illustrating, formatting, or recording a story, and no more juggling separate AI tools by hand — one paste-in, one output.
The Steroid (the magic moment):
The story comes alive with a consistent character, warm narration, and synced illustrations in under 60 seconds — turning a plain paragraph into something that feels like a real storybook was made just for that story.
2c. Goals & Non-Goals
Goals
Prove the core AI pipeline (text → scenes → illustrations → narration → player) works end-to-end without manual intervention.
Deliver a demo-ready experience that looks polished enough to show a teacher or parent, not just a technical proof of concept.
Keep the same illustrated character recognizable across all scenes in a given story.
Complete generation in under 60 seconds so the experience feels immediate, not like a batch job.
Non-Goals
Lip-sync or gesture animation on the avatar — deferred to a future version.
Comprehension quizzes, vocabulary activities, teacher dashboards, analytics, or a saved story library — deferred, not part of this build.
Speech-to-text/audio story input and multi-language support — deferred, text input only for this version.

3. Requirements
User Journey 1: Teacher or parent turning a story into a presentation
Context: This is the core journey for the MVP — the entire demo depends on this flow working reliably, quickly, and without manual intervention.
Sub-journey: Inputting a story
[P0] User can paste or type a story into a text input, with no login required.
[P0] User can submit the story to begin generation with a single action.
[P2] User can upload a story as a simple text file instead of pasting.
Sub-journey: Generating the presentation
[P0] System can split the submitted story into 3–5 scenes (beginning/middle/end structure).
[P0] System can generate one illustration per scene, keeping the same character description across all scenes.
[P0] System can generate narration audio per scene, using a warm, slower-paced voice.
[P0] System can complete generation without manual intervention on varied input stories.
[P1] System can complete the full generation process in under 60 seconds.
[P2] System can detect and flag a likely character-consistency mismatch between scenes.
Sub-journey: Viewing the presentation
[P0] User can play the presentation scene by scene, with illustration, narration, and text displayed together.
[P0] User can advance between scenes, automatically or via a play/next control.
[P1] User can see the story text highlighted in sync with the narration audio.
[P1] User can see a static or simple 2-pose avatar synced to audio playback.
[P2] User can restart or replay a scene.
User Journey 2: Presenter demoing NarrateMe live
Context: Since this is a 2-week MVP intended primarily for a live demo, the demo experience itself is a first-class journey, not an afterthought.
Sub-journey: Preparing for a live demo
[P0] Presenter can access at least one pre-generated backup story in case live generation fails during the demo.
[P1] Presenter can trigger generation live, in front of an audience, with visible progress/loading state.
[P2] Presenter can choose between multiple pre-generated backup stories.
4. Appendix
Known accepted risk: character consistency across DALL-E-generated scene illustrations may drift; mitigation is a strong, persistent character-description prompt reused across every scene's image generation call, not a technical/model-level fix. Flagged as future work rather than solved in this 2-week build.
Future scope (post-MVP, not this build): avatar lip-sync/gesture animation, comprehension quizzes, teacher dashboard, saved story library, voice cloning, multi-language support, background music/sound design, analytics.
Team: 4 members across Backend/AI Engineer, AI/Visual Engineer, Audio Engineer, and Frontend/UX Lead roles, working in parallel over a 14-day sprint.
